Total software abundance is usually told as an economic story: the destruction of white collar jobs, a consumer credit crisis—all of which may be disastrous. Beneath that however the entire market is shifting in a very positive direction. Software is big and cumbersome and in a competitive environment the best products can only be delivere by massive teams that are constantly tweaking and improving their product. The price that these companies asked in return for this product? You: your email, your phone number, your attention, your habits, a complete record of your usage of their product, as well as every bit of information you put into their program, forever. 

I use the word “ask” because they don’t need it. They take it because they can, the price for having the marginally best product on the market. The market values this a lot, when a marginal advantage over the competition very well could mean dominating the market….
